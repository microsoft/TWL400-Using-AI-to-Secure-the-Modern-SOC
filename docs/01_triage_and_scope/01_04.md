# Task 01.04 — Recognize why signal overload hides the chain

## Introduction

You found the chain in about half an hour — but you were looking for it, with Copilot correlating across layers for you. Acesa's analysts weren't, and couldn't. This short analytical task names *why* the attack stayed invisible, because that diagnosis is half of the module's executive deliverable.

## Description

You will contrast the unified incident with the raw alert queue and articulate the specific reasons normal triage misses this attack — the finding that seeds the **Proof Through Scenario**.

## Success Criteria

- You can name why the chain is missed under normal triage _(Validation: overload recognized)_
- Your explanation cites both **signal fragmentation** (across products/consoles) and **low individual severity** (alert fatigue)
- You note at least one **cross-layer** reason specific to the AI stage

### 02: Name the failure conditions

Write the diagnosis.

<details markdown="block">
<summary><strong>Expand this section for detailed steps</strong></summary>

1. In your notes, capture 2–3 sentences naming why the chain is missed. A strong answer covers:

   - **Fragmentation** — the signals live in five different consoles (Entra, Defender for Office 365, the AI-threat surface, Defender for Identity, Defender for Endpoint); no analyst is watching all five at once.
   - **Low severity / alert fatigue** — each signal is individually low or informational and competes with dozens of others; under Acesa's 50–75-tool load, they get triaged away.
   - **A cross-layer blind spot** — nothing connects the AI-layer activity (poisoned grounding → Refund Agent probing) to the identity and endpoint signals, so the most consequential stage is the most invisible.

2. Note the business consequence in one line: roughly **75 minutes** elapsed from compromise to the attacker reaching high-value AI assets, with no coordinated response.

   {: .tip }
   > Keep this diagnosis tight and quotable. It becomes the "why detection failed" half of the Proof Through Scenario you assemble in Exercise 04.

</details>

## Summary

You've named exactly why Acesa's SOC missed a full compromise: the right signals, fragmented across consoles, each too quiet to chase, with the AI layer disconnected from everything else. With the incident scoped, the entities anchored, and the failure diagnosed, you're ready to reconstruct the full lifecycle — and find every point where detection and response should have fired — in Exercise 02.
