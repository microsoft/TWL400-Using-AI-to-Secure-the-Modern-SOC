# Task 01.03 — Extract entities and assemble the initial timeline

## Introduction

A narrative is persuasive; a list of entities and an ordered timeline are *actionable*. Before you reconstruct the attack in Exercise 02, you need firm anchors — the specific users, devices, resources, and indicators, in the order they appear — so your hunting queries have something concrete to pivot on.

## Description

You will extract the key entities from the incident and the Copilot narrative, and assemble an initial, ordered timeline of the attack.

## Success Criteria

- Key actors and indicators are captured _(Validation: entities extracted)_
- An ordered initial timeline is recorded _(Validation: timeline extracted)_
- The entity list spans all five stages (identity, AI, data, infrastructure, endpoint)

### 02: Assemble the initial timeline

Put the entities in order.

<details markdown="block">
<summary><strong>Expand this section for detailed steps</strong></summary>

1. Ask Copilot to order the events:

   ```text
   Build a chronological timeline of this incident. For each event give the
   timestamp, the layer, the entity, and a one-line description.
   ```

2. Sanity-check the result against the incident alert timestamps. The seeded events are **rebased to land just before your session**, and each stage's `TimeGenerated` is **randomized across the lab window** and shuffled in among the ~25 decoys — so treat the times below as the *relative* shape of the chain (initial access → poisoning → AI probing → infra reach → endpoint execution), not as fixed wall-clock values. Your initial timeline should resemble:

   | Order | Layer | Event |
   |-------|-------|-------|
   | 1 | Identity | Impossible-travel sign-in for Mariya Petrova after AiTM phishing |
   | 2 | Data / AI | Poisoned policy document saved into the Refund Policy Knowledge Base |
   | 3 | AI | Prompt-injection / jailbreak attempts against the Refund Agent |
   | 4 | Infrastructure | Anomalous token/compute requests by `sp-refund-agent-inference` |
   | 5 | Endpoint | `update_check.ps1` executes on OPS-LT-0427 (synthetic asset) |

   {: .note }
   > This is an **initial** timeline built from the correlated alerts. Because the seeder randomizes timestamps and interleaves the chain with decoys, when you hunt in Exercise 02 you'll widen your KQL time windows to span the whole lab window rather than a tight per-stage range. It has gaps and assumptions — you'll confirm and tighten it with KQL hunting in Exercise 02.

</details>

### 03: Save the anchors

Record what reconstruction will pivot on.

<details markdown="block">
<summary><strong>Expand this section for detailed steps</strong></summary>

1. Save the entity table and the initial timeline to your running notes.

2. Flag the two entities most likely to unlock the rest: **Mariya Petrova** (identity pivot) and **`sp-refund-agent-inference`** (infrastructure pivot). You'll lead with these in Exercise 02's hunting.

</details>

## Summary

You now have a concrete entity list spanning all five stages and an ordered initial timeline. These anchors turn the next exercise from open-ended hunting into targeted reconstruction. Before you move on, look back at the raw queue one more time — ~25 decoys with the chain laced through it — and articulate why a human analyst would have missed all of this.
