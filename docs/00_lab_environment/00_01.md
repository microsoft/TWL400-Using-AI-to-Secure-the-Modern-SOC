# Task 00.01 — Sign in and verify console access

## Introduction

Everything in this lab happens in the browser, inside Zava's pre-provisioned tenant _(illustrative tenant `zava.onmicrosoft.com` — confirm at build)_. Before the investigation starts, confirm you can reach the three surfaces you'll rely on, so you don't lose investigation time to an access problem mid-incident.

## Description

You will sign in with your assigned lab credentials, complete multifactor authentication, and confirm access to the **Microsoft Defender portal**, **Microsoft Sentinel** (within the Defender portal), and **standalone Microsoft Security Copilot**.

## Success Criteria

- You are signed in to the Microsoft Defender portal and can see the **Incidents** queue
- You can open **Advanced hunting** and confirm the Sentinel-backed tables are present
- You can open standalone Security Copilot and submit a prompt

## Key Tasks

### 01: Sign in to the Defender portal

Sign in to the unified SecOps console where you'll spend most of the lab.

<details markdown="block">
<summary><strong>Expand this section for detailed steps</strong></summary>

1. Open a private/incognito browser window (so you don't collide with any personal Microsoft session) and navigate to [https://security.microsoft.com](https://security.microsoft.com){:target="_blank"}.

2. Sign in with the lab credentials your facilitator provided (for example, `analyst@zava.onmicrosoft.com`). Complete the multifactor authentication prompt.

3. When the **Microsoft Defender** portal loads, confirm you land on the home dashboard.

   {: .note }
   > 📷 **Screenshot to capture:** the Defender portal home page after sign-in, left navigation visible.

</details>

### 02: Confirm the Incidents queue and Advanced hunting

Confirm you can reach the two places you'll investigate from.

<details markdown="block">
<summary><strong>Expand this section for detailed steps</strong></summary>

1. In the left navigation, select **Incidents & alerts > Incidents**. Confirm the queue lists open incidents.

2. In the left navigation, select **Investigation & response > Hunting > Advanced hunting** (Microsoft Sentinel is unified into this Defender portal experience for the lab).

3. Run the following query to confirm the Sentinel-backed tables are present and populated:

   ```kusto
   union withsource=TableName
       SigninLogs, EmailEvents, DeviceProcessEvents, AzureActivity,
       SocLabEvents_CL
   | summarize Rows = count() by TableName
   | order by TableName asc
   ```

   You should see a non-zero row count for each table.

   {: .warning }
   > `SocLabEvents_CL` is the **primary custom injection table** for this lab — it carries the seeded commodity signals (identity, email/poisoning, infrastructure, and endpoint) **and the ~25 decoy noise event-sets** that bury the attack chain. The real AI-attack evidence (Stage 3) is not in this table — it lands as a native **Defender for Cloud — AI threat protection** alert you'll query via `SecurityAlert` / `AlertEvidence` later. The exact name and schema of `SocLabEvents_CL` are confirmed during the build, and the table appears only once its Data Collection Rule has finished provisioning; if it's missing, tell your facilitator before continuing.

</details>

### 03: Open standalone Security Copilot

Confirm Security Copilot — your cross-layer reasoning spine for the lab — is reachable.

<details markdown="block">
<summary><strong>Expand this section for detailed steps</strong></summary>

1. In a new browser tab, navigate to [https://securitycopilot.microsoft.com](https://securitycopilot.microsoft.com){:target="_blank"} and sign in with the same lab credentials.

2. At the prompt bar, submit a warm-up prompt to confirm the session works:

   ```text
   What can you help me investigate in this tenant?
   ```

3. Confirm you get a response and that the Defender XDR and Sentinel plugins show as enabled (icon near the prompt bar).

   {: .note }
   > 📷 **Screenshot to capture:** the standalone Security Copilot prompt bar with the response to the warm-up prompt and the enabled plugins indicator.

</details>

## Summary

You are signed in and have confirmed access to the Defender portal, the Sentinel-backed hunting tables, and standalone Security Copilot. With your tools open, you're ready to understand the scenario you're walking into.
