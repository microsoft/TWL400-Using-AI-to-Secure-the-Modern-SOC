# Task 03.04 — Document residual risk

## Introduction

Containment is not the same as "safe." You revoked the sessions, disabled the service principal, quarantined the poisoned document, and restricted the inference deployment — but every one of those actions stops the attack *going forward*. None of them undoes what already happened in the roughly 75 minutes the attacker had, and none of them fully answers what the poisoned grounding already changed or where the stolen access already reached.

A mature SOC names that gap out loud. This short task captures the **residual risk** that remains after containment — the open questions, the unbounded blast radius, the things you cannot yet prove are clean — so the response closes with an honest risk statement rather than a false "all clear." That statement is part of the executive deliverable.

## Description

You will document the residual risk that remains after the coordinated containment: the unknown blast radius of the poisoned grounding on prior Refund Agent outputs, whether the attacker retained any tokens or access, and any downstream data exposure — each with a recommended follow-up.

## Success Criteria

- **Residual risk is documented** _(Validation: residual risk documented)_
- The note covers, at minimum: AI-layer blast radius (poisoned grounding's effect on prior outputs), identity/token persistence, and downstream data exposure
- Each residual risk has an owner and a recommended next step (not just a description)
- The note is captured in a form ready to feed the Proof Through Scenario in Exercise 04

## Key Tasks

### 01: Enumerate what containment did not resolve

Name the open risk, layer by layer.

<details markdown="block">
<summary><strong>Expand this section for detailed steps</strong></summary>

1. Walk each layer you contained and ask "what is still unknown or still exposed?" Use the impacted-interaction list from Task 03.01 (Purview DSPM for AI) and the lifecycle from Exercise 02 as your evidence. You should arrive at something like:

   | Residual risk | Layer | Why it remains after containment |
   |---------------|-------|----------------------------------|
   | Unknown blast radius of the poisoned grounding on **prior** Refund Agent outputs | Data / AI | Quarantining the document stops future poisoned answers, but refund decisions or guidance already produced between 08:47 and containment may have been shaped by it. The full set of affected outputs — and any actions taken on them — is not yet bounded. |
   | Possible **token / access persistence** | Identity | Revoking sessions and disabling the service principal closes the known paths, but it is not yet proven the attacker did not establish other persistence (e.g., a registered MFA method, an app consent, a secondary token) during the dwell window. |
   | **Downstream data exposure** | Data | The poisoned grounding sits in the Refund Policy Knowledge Base, which contains customer-refund context. Whether the attacker exfiltrated or the agent surfaced sensitive refund data through the manipulated answers is not yet confirmed. |
   | **Lateral reach** beyond the known entities | Infrastructure / Identity | The service principal moved toward `rg-acesa-ai-prod`; it is not yet confirmed whether it touched anything else before it was disabled. |

2. Be explicit about the worst of these: the AI-layer blast radius is the residual risk most likely to be *underestimated*, because the poisoned outputs left almost no native detection (the Exercise 02 blind spot) — the only record is the DSPM-for-AI interaction trail.

### 02: Assign owners and next steps, then write the risk statement

Turn open risk into tracked work and one quotable paragraph.

<details markdown="block">
<summary><strong>Expand this section for detailed steps</strong></summary>

1. Give every residual risk an **owner** and a **recommended next step** so the note is actionable rather than just a worry list. For example:

   | Residual risk | Owner _(illustrative — confirm at build)_ | Recommended next step |
   |---------------|-------------------------------------------|-----------------------|
   | Poisoned-grounding blast radius | **Nancy** (Data Security & Governance Lead) / **Amy** (App/AI Security Lead) | Review every DSPM-for-AI-flagged interaction; flag and re-handle any refund decision influenced by the poisoned content. |
   | Token / access persistence | **Phillip** (Identity & SecOps Lead) | Audit Mariya's account and the service principal for added MFA methods, app consents, and secondary tokens; confirm no other risky sign-ins. |
   | Downstream data exposure | **Nancy** (Data Security & Governance Lead) | Run a Purview audit/eDiscovery scope over the Refund Policy Knowledge Base for exfiltration indicators during the window. |
   | Lateral reach | **Quinton** (Infrastructure & Architecture Lead) | Confirm the service principal's activity in `rg-acesa-ai-prod` and adjacent resources; verify nothing else was accessed. |

2. Write a tight **residual-risk statement** — 2–3 sentences a CISO can read aloud: the attack is contained across identity, data, and infrastructure, but the blast radius of the poisoned grounding on prior Refund Agent outputs is not yet fully bounded, token persistence and downstream data exposure are unconfirmed, and follow-up is owned and tracked. Save it with the response artifacts.

   {: .tip }
   > This statement is the honest counterpart to "we caught it." It becomes part of the **Proof Through Scenario** in Exercise 04 — the proof is not just that the AI-driven SOC contained the chain, but that it can state precisely what risk remains and who owns closing it.

</details>

## Summary

You closed the response with an honest accounting of what remains: the unbounded blast radius of the poisoned grounding on prior Refund Agent outputs, unconfirmed token persistence, and possible downstream data exposure — each assigned an owner and a next step, and distilled into a quotable risk statement. Containment spans identity, data, and infrastructure; the residual risk is named rather than hidden. With the coordinated response executed, operationalized, bounded by an explicit autonomy boundary, and closed with a residual-risk note, you have everything you need to assemble the Proof Through Scenario in Exercise 04.
