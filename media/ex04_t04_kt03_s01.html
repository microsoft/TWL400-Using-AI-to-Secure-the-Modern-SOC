Component 0 — Executive Summary

One-paragraph opening — CISO / CAIO audience

A five-stage attack moved from a stolen employee credential to active operations against Zava's production AI infrastructure in approximately 75 minutes, passing through identity, data, AI, cloud infrastructure, and endpoint in sequence — with each stage generating a separate, low-severity alert in a different tool and every signal competing for attention among roughly 25 other incidents. A well-staffed security team managing 50 to 75 fragmented tools could not realistically correlate those signals at the pace the attacker moved; the AI-layer alert fired and was lost in the noise, the poisoned data source was never connected to the identity compromise that enabled it, and 75 minutes of unchecked access to a production AI system was the result. An AI-driven security operations model changes the outcome at every step: it correlates the five-stage chain into a single incident automatically, surfaces the AI-layer signals that a conventional model drops, and executes coordinated containment across identity, data, and infrastructure in minutes rather than hours — with human approval preserved for the actions that carry business risk.

Component 3 — Business-Impact Narrative

Executive framing — CISO and CAIO perspectives

Vikram Nair · CISO

A single stolen credential cascaded through five security layers in 75 minutes because no tool in the stack was watching the connections between layers — only the events within each one. The SOC managed 25 concurrent incidents across 50–75 tools; the cross-layer chain was statistically indistinguishable from background noise. That's not a resourcing failure. It's a structural one: the current model cannot produce a coordinated response faster than the attacker can move.

An AI-driven SOC resolves the structural problem. It correlates cross-layer signals automatically, cuts mean-time-to-detect from hours to minutes, and executes containment — credential revocation, data quarantine, playbook trigger — without waiting for a human to assemble the picture. Human judgment stays in the loop for decisions that carry business risk. The operational model doesn't require replacing the stack; it requires adding correlation and autonomous response as a layer above it.

Michelle Osei · CAIO

The attacker's chosen pivot point was the AI layer — specifically, the grounding documents that sp-refund-agent uses to answer customer queries. Poisoning that document didn't require breaking any AI-specific security control, because no AI-specific security control existed. The pipeline ingested the document as trusted input, and the agent answered accordingly for as long as the document sat in the container.

The implication for AI governance is direct: every production AI system that ingests external data is a potential exfiltration channel, and the signals it generates when abused — anomalous inference patterns, unexpected grounding sources, response drift — are invisible to a SOC that monitors at the identity, network, and endpoint layers. AI security requires AI-aware monitoring. The Proof Through Scenario is the evidence that gap is not theoretical.
